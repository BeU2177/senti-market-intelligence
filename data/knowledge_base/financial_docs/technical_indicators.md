# Technical Analysis Indicators & Signals

## Relative Strength Index (RSI)
The Relative Strength Index (RSI) measures the speed and change of price movements on a scale of 0 to 100.
Traditional interpretations:
- RSI above 70 indicates an overbought condition, suggesting potential price pullbacks.
- RSI below 30 indicates an oversold condition, suggesting potential price rebounds.
- Centerline crossover at 50 signals shifting bullish (>50) or bearish (<50) momentum.

## Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD)
MACD tracks the relationship between two exponential moving averages (typically 12-day and 26-day EMA).
- MACD Line = 12-period EMA - 26-period EMA.
- Signal Line = 9-period EMA of MACD Line.
- Histogram = MACD Line - Signal Line.
Crossovers above the signal line indicate bullish momentum; crossovers below signal bearish momentum.

## Bollinger Bands & Volatility
Bollinger Bands consist of a middle N-period moving average (SMA 20) with upper and lower bands set 2 standard deviations away.
- Bandwidth contraction ("Squeeze") indicates impending high volatility moves.
- Price touching upper band indicates high relative price; lower band indicates low relative price.

## Average True Range (ATR)
ATR measures historical price volatility by considering gap openings and daily high-low ranges. Higher ATR indicates higher market volatility.
